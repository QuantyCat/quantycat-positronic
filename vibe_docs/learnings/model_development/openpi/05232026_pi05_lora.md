## Key Findings

### j0 shoulder_pan — excellent
Slope ~1.0, correlation 0.965, sign agreement 1.0. Essentially perfect. Don't touch.

### j1 shoulder_lift — very good
Sign agreement 1.0, correlation 0.969. Slight undershoot (slope 0.894) but overall strong. Could try gain 1.1 if live reach is visibly short.

### j2 elbow_flex — problem joint
Perfect sign agreement (1.0) but correlation only **0.622** and slope 0.617. The gap between high sign agreement and low correlation is the key diagnostic: this is not just amplitude damping — it points to timing/phase error, inconsistency across windows, or the model averaging across multiple elbow strategies in the dataset.

Gain calibration confirmed that scalar gain makes it worse:

| j2 gain | slope | norm MAE |
|---:|---:|---:|
| 1.0 | 0.617 | 0.091 |
| 1.2 | 0.741 | 0.133 |
| 1.4 | 0.864 | 0.214 |
| 1.6 | 0.988 | 0.304 |

Slope-correcting gain (1.6) triples MAE — scalar amplification is the wrong fix.

**Likely root causes:**
1. Gravity/servo lag made elbow labels inconsistent — model sees visually similar states with different commanded targets and averages them into smaller actions
2. Slow demos + pauses encourage conservative predictions — same visual neighborhood maps to both "continue elbow motion" and "pause/settle"
3. Timing variation — demos start elbow extension at different times, so frame-by-frame slope drops due to phase shift
4. j2 is coupled to shoulder motion — model may average across multiple reach-to-place strategies
5. Command limits/clipping in training data — if elbow targets were frequently clipped, the action distribution may not represent clean physical deltas

### j3 wrist_flex — moderate concern
Highest normalized MAE (0.134) and a large negative bias offset (centered_sign_center = -0.149). Wrist flex is systematically offset, not just noisy. Sign agreement 0.957 is still good. Do not apply gain until bias source is understood.

### j4 wrist_roll — moderate
Direction good (sign agreement 0.997), amplitude undershot (slope 0.779, correlation 0.847). Lower priority than j2/j3.
